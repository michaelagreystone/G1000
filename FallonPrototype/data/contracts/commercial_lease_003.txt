COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

This Commercial Lease Agreement ("Lease") is entered into as of April 1, 2024, by and between:

LANDLORD: Fallon Seaport Office LLC, a Delaware limited liability company, with principal offices at 1 Seaport Lane, Suite 500, Boston, MA 02210 ("Landlord")

TENANT: TechVentures Inc., a Delaware corporation, with principal offices at 888 Boylston Street, Boston, MA 02199 ("Tenant")

PREMISES: Suite 1500-1800, comprising approximately 45,000 rentable square feet on floors 15 through 18 of the building known as One Seaport Square, located at 60 Seaport Boulevard, Boston, Massachusetts 02210 (the "Building")

ARTICLE 1 - BASIC LEASE TERMS

1.1 Premises: Approximately 45,000 rentable square feet as shown on Exhibit A

1.2 Building: One Seaport Square, 60 Seaport Boulevard, Boston, MA 02210

1.3 Lease Term: Twelve (12) years

1.4 Commencement Date: October 1, 2024

1.5 Expiration Date: September 30, 2036

1.6 Base Rent:
    Years 1-3:    $95.00 per rentable square foot ($4,275,000.00 annually)
    Years 4-6:    $100.00 per rentable square foot ($4,500,000.00 annually)
    Years 7-9:    $105.00 per rentable square foot ($4,725,000.00 annually)
    Years 10-12:  $110.00 per rentable square foot ($4,950,000.00 annually)

1.7 Tenant's Proportionate Share: 12.5% (based on 360,000 RSF building)

1.8 Security Deposit: $1,425,000.00 (equivalent to four months' initial Base Rent)

1.9 Tenant Improvement Allowance: $85.00 per rentable square foot ($3,825,000.00)

1.10 Parking: Thirty (30) unreserved parking spaces at $450.00 per space per month

ARTICLE 2 - RENT

2.1 Base Rent. Tenant shall pay Base Rent in equal monthly installments in advance on the first day of each month.

2.2 Additional Rent. Tenant shall pay as Additional Rent:
    (a) Tenant's Proportionate Share of Operating Expenses in excess of Base Year 2024
    (b) Tenant's Proportionate Share of Real Estate Taxes in excess of Base Year 2024

2.3 Operating Expenses. Operating Expenses include all costs of operating, maintaining, repairing, and managing the Building and Common Areas, including but not limited to:
    (a) Insurance
    (b) Utilities (common areas)
    (c) Security
    (d) Janitorial services
    (e) Landscaping
    (f) Management fees (capped at 3% of gross revenues)
    (g) Repairs and maintenance
    (h) Capital improvements (amortized over useful life)

2.4 Operating Expense Cap. Annual increases in Controllable Operating Expenses shall be capped at five percent (5%) per year on a cumulative, compounding basis.

ARTICLE 3 - TENANT IMPROVEMENTS

3.1 Allowance. Landlord shall provide a Tenant Improvement Allowance of $85.00 per rentable square foot ($3,825,000.00) toward the cost of Tenant's initial improvements.

3.2 Excess Costs. Tenant shall be responsible for all improvement costs in excess of the Allowance.

3.3 Plans and Approval. Tenant shall submit final construction drawings to Landlord for approval within sixty (60) days of Lease execution. Landlord shall not unreasonably withhold approval.

3.4 Construction. Tenant improvements shall be constructed by Landlord's designated contractor, with Tenant having approval rights over subcontractors.

ARTICLE 4 - RENEWAL OPTIONS

4.1 Renewal Terms. Tenant shall have two (2) options to renew this Lease for additional terms of five (5) years each.

4.2 Notice. Tenant must exercise each renewal option by giving written notice to Landlord not less than twelve (12) months prior to the expiration of the then-current term.

4.3 Renewal Rent. Base Rent for each renewal term shall be the greater of:
    (a) The Base Rent in effect during the final year of the preceding term; or
    (b) Ninety-five percent (95%) of the then-current Fair Market Rent

ARTICLE 5 - RIGHT OF FIRST OFFER

5.1 ROFO Space. If any space on floors 14 or 19 of the Building becomes available during the Lease term, Landlord shall first offer such space to Tenant.

5.2 Notice and Response. Landlord shall provide written notice of availability, and Tenant shall have fifteen (15) business days to accept or decline.

5.3 Terms. ROFO Space shall be leased at Fair Market Rent for a term coterminous with this Lease.

ARTICLE 6 - TERMINATION OPTION

6.1 Early Termination. Tenant shall have a one-time right to terminate this Lease effective at the end of Year 7, upon:
    (a) Providing written notice not less than twelve (12) months prior to termination
    (b) Payment of a termination fee equal to: unamortized Tenant Improvement Allowance, unamortized brokerage commissions, and three (3) months' Base Rent then in effect

ARTICLE 7 - ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING

7.1 Consent Required. Tenant may not assign this Lease or sublet any portion of the Premises without Landlord's prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

7.2 Recapture Right. Landlord shall have the right to recapture the Premises if Tenant proposes to assign this Lease or sublet more than fifty percent (50%) of the Premises.

7.3 Profit Sharing. If Tenant assigns or sublets at rent exceeding the Base Rent hereunder, fifty percent (50%) of such excess (after deduction of Tenant's reasonable costs) shall be paid to Landlord.

7.4 Permitted Transfers. Notwithstanding the above, Tenant may assign or sublet to any affiliate or successor by merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets without Landlord consent.

ARTICLE 8 - DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

8.1 Tenant Default. The following constitute Events of Default:
    (a) Failure to pay rent within five (5) days of written notice
    (b) Failure to perform other obligations within thirty (30) days of written notice
    (c) Bankruptcy or insolvency

8.2 Landlord Remedies. Upon default, Landlord may:
    (a) Terminate this Lease
    (b) Re-enter and relet the Premises
    (c) Accelerate rent through the Lease term
    (d) Recover all damages permitted by law

8.3 Landlord Default. Landlord shall be in default if it fails to perform its obligations within thirty (30) days of written notice (or longer if cure cannot reasonably be completed in 30 days and Landlord is diligently pursuing cure).

8.4 Tenant Remedies. Upon Landlord default, Tenant may:
    (a) Perform the obligation and offset costs against rent
    (b) Seek specific performance or damages

ARTICLE 9 - INSURANCE

9.1 Tenant Insurance. Tenant shall maintain:
    (a) Commercial general liability: $3,000,000 per occurrence, $5,000,000 aggregate
    (b) Property insurance covering Tenant's improvements and personal property
    (c) Workers' compensation as required by law

9.2 Landlord Insurance. Landlord shall maintain property insurance covering the Building in full replacement cost.

9.3 Waiver of Subrogation. Landlord and Tenant each waive any right of recovery against the other for losses covered by insurance.

ARTICLE 10 - GOVERNING LAW

10.1 This Lease shall be governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.
