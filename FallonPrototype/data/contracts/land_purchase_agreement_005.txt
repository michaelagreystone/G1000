PURCHASE AND SALE AGREEMENT

This Purchase and Sale Agreement ("Agreement") is made as of May 1, 2024, by and between:

SELLER: Nashville Downtown Partners, LLC, a Tennessee limited liability company ("Seller")

PURCHASER: Fallon Nashville Development LLC, a Delaware limited liability company, or its permitted assigns ("Purchaser")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Seller is the owner of approximately 2.5 acres of undeveloped land located at 1000 Church Street, Nashville, Tennessee 37203, together with all improvements, rights, and appurtenances thereto (collectively, the "Property");

WHEREAS, Purchaser desires to purchase the Property for the development of a mixed-use project consisting of hotel, residential, and retail components;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 - PURCHASE PRICE AND PAYMENT

1.1 Purchase Price. The purchase price for the Property shall be Twenty-Eight Million Dollars ($28,000,000.00) (the "Purchase Price").

1.2 Earnest Money Deposit.
    (a) Initial Deposit: Within five (5) business days of execution, Purchaser shall deposit One Million Dollars ($1,000,000.00) with Escrow Agent.
    (b) Additional Deposit: Upon expiration of the Due Diligence Period, Purchaser shall deposit an additional One Million Dollars ($1,000,000.00).
    (c) Total Deposit: Two Million Dollars ($2,000,000.00)

1.3 Payment at Closing. The Purchase Price, less the Earnest Money Deposit, shall be paid in immediately available funds at Closing.

1.4 Escrow Agent. First American Title Insurance Company, 222 2nd Avenue North, Nashville, TN 37201

ARTICLE 2 - DUE DILIGENCE

2.1 Due Diligence Period. Purchaser shall have ninety (90) days from the Effective Date to conduct its due diligence investigations (the "Due Diligence Period").

2.2 Access. During the Due Diligence Period, Purchaser and its representatives may:
    (a) Enter the Property for inspections, surveys, and environmental assessments
    (b) Review all books, records, contracts, and documents relating to the Property
    (c) Obtain title commitments and surveys
    (d) Conduct Phase I and Phase II environmental assessments
    (e) Evaluate zoning and entitlement matters

2.3 Due Diligence Deliverables. Within ten (10) days of execution, Seller shall deliver:
    (a) Existing surveys and title policies
    (b) Environmental reports
    (c) Geotechnical reports
    (d) Existing contracts affecting the Property
    (e) Tax bills for the past three years
    (f) Zoning letters and entitlement documents

2.4 Termination Right. Purchaser may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason by written notice to Seller prior to expiration of the Due Diligence Period. Upon termination, the Initial Deposit shall be returned to Purchaser.

2.5 Deposit Goes Hard. Upon expiration of the Due Diligence Period without termination, the Earnest Money Deposit shall become non-refundable except as otherwise provided herein.

ARTICLE 3 - TITLE AND SURVEY

3.1 Title Commitment. Within thirty (30) days of execution, Seller shall deliver a title commitment from the Title Company.

3.2 Survey. Purchaser may obtain an ALTA/NSPS survey at Purchaser's expense.

3.3 Title Objections. Purchaser may object to any title or survey matters within forty-five (45) days of execution. Seller shall have fifteen (15) days to cure or agree to cure objections. If Seller is unable or unwilling to cure, Purchaser may:
    (a) Accept title as-is and proceed to Closing
    (b) Terminate this Agreement and receive return of the Deposit

3.4 Permitted Exceptions. Title shall be conveyed subject only to:
    (a) Current real property taxes not yet due
    (b) Zoning ordinances
    (c) Matters approved or waived by Purchaser

ARTICLE 4 - REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

4.1 Seller's Representations. Seller represents and warrants:
    (a) Seller has full power and authority to execute this Agreement and convey the Property
    (b) There is no pending or threatened litigation affecting the Property
    (c) Seller has not received notice of any environmental violations
    (d) There are no contracts or leases affecting the Property that will survive Closing
    (e) No condemnation proceedings are pending or threatened
    (f) Seller is not a foreign person under FIRPTA

4.2 Purchaser's Representations. Purchaser represents and warrants:
    (a) Purchaser has full power and authority to execute this Agreement
    (b) Purchaser has adequate financial resources to complete the transaction

4.3 Survival. Representations and warranties shall survive Closing for a period of twelve (12) months.

ARTICLE 5 - CONDITIONS PRECEDENT TO CLOSING

5.1 Purchaser's Conditions.
    (a) All representations and warranties remain true
    (b) Seller has performed all obligations
    (c) No material adverse change to the Property
    (d) Title policy issued in accordance with this Agreement

5.2 Seller's Conditions.
    (a) All representations and warranties remain true
    (b) Purchaser has performed all obligations
    (c) Purchaser delivers the Purchase Price

ARTICLE 6 - CLOSING

6.1 Closing Date. Closing shall occur within thirty (30) days following expiration of the Due Diligence Period, or such other date as mutually agreed (the "Closing Date").

6.2 Seller's Closing Deliveries.
    (a) Special warranty deed
    (b) Bill of sale for personal property
    (c) Assignment of contracts and permits
    (d) Non-foreign affidavit (FIRPTA)
    (e) Owner's title affidavit
    (f) All keys, codes, and access devices

6.3 Purchaser's Closing Deliveries.
    (a) Purchase Price in immediately available funds
    (b) Evidence of authority
    (c) Such other documents reasonably required

6.4 Closing Costs.
    (a) Seller pays: Transfer taxes, recording fees for deed, Seller's attorneys' fees
    (b) Purchaser pays: Title insurance premium, survey costs, Purchaser's attorneys' fees
    (c) Prorations: Real property taxes prorated as of Closing

ARTICLE 7 - DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

7.1 Purchaser Default. If Purchaser defaults after the Deposit becomes non-refundable, Seller's sole remedy shall be to retain the Earnest Money Deposit as liquidated damages.

7.2 Seller Default. If Seller defaults:
    (a) Purchaser may terminate and receive return of the Deposit plus documented out-of-pocket expenses up to $250,000; or
    (b) Purchaser may seek specific performance

ARTICLE 8 - ASSIGNMENT

8.1 Purchaser may assign this Agreement to any affiliate or to an entity formed for the purpose of acquiring the Property, provided that:
    (a) Purchaser provides written notice to Seller
    (b) Purchaser remains liable for all obligations hereunder

ARTICLE 9 - MISCELLANEOUS

9.1 Confidentiality. The terms of this Agreement are confidential and shall not be disclosed except as required by law or with the other party's consent.

9.2 AS-IS. Purchaser is acquiring the Property "AS-IS, WHERE-IS" with all faults, subject to Purchaser's due diligence investigations.

9.3 Brokers. Seller shall pay a commission to CBRE equal to two percent (2%) of the Purchase Price. Each party represents it has not dealt with any other broker.

ARTICLE 10 - GOVERNING LAW

10.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Tennessee.
