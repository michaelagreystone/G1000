LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY OPERATING AGREEMENT
OF
FALLON-INSTITUTIONAL PARTNER JV, LLC

This Limited Liability Company Operating Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2024, by and between:

MANAGING MEMBER: Fallon Seaport Development LLC, a Delaware limited liability company ("Fallon" or "Managing Member"), with offices at 1 Seaport Lane, Suite 500, Boston, MA 02210

INVESTOR MEMBER: Metropolitan Real Estate Partners Fund VII, LP, a Delaware limited partnership ("Investor" or "Limited Partner"), with offices at 1345 Avenue of the Americas, New York, NY 10105

The Company formed hereby is FALLON-INSTITUTIONAL PARTNER JV, LLC (the "Company").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Members desire to form a Delaware limited liability company for the purpose of acquiring, developing, and operating a mixed-use development project consisting of 450 residential units and 25,000 square feet of retail space located at 500 Seaport Boulevard, Boston, Massachusetts (the "Project"); and

WHEREAS, the Members desire to set forth their rights and obligations with respect to the Company and the Project;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Members agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 - FORMATION AND PURPOSE

1.1 Formation. The Company was formed as a Delaware limited liability company on February 15, 2024.

1.2 Purpose. The purpose of the Company is to acquire, develop, own, operate, and ultimately sell or refinance the Project.

ARTICLE 2 - CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

2.1 Total Project Capitalization. The total equity capitalization for the Project shall be One Hundred Twenty Million Dollars ($120,000,000.00).

2.2 Member Contributions.
    (a) Investor shall contribute ninety percent (90%) of all required Capital Contributions.
    (b) Managing Member shall contribute ten percent (10%) of all required Capital Contributions.

2.3 Initial Capital Contributions.
    (a) Investor: $108,000,000.00
    (b) Managing Member: $12,000,000.00

ARTICLE 3 - ALLOCATION OF PROFITS AND LOSSES

3.1 Allocation of Profits. Profits shall be allocated as follows:
    (a) First, to Members in proportion to their respective Capital Account deficits, if any
    (b) Second, to Members in accordance with the distribution waterfall set forth in Article 4

ARTICLE 4 - DISTRIBUTIONS AND WATERFALL

4.1 Distribution Waterfall. Available Cash Flow shall be distributed as follows:

    TIER 1 - Return of Capital:
    First, to Investor until Investor has received distributions equal to its Capital Contributions.

    TIER 2 - Preferred Return:
    Second, to Investor until Investor has received a cumulative, non-compounded preferred return of nine percent (9%) per annum on its unreturned Capital Contributions.

    TIER 3 - Catch-Up:
    Third, one hundred percent (100%) to Managing Member until Managing Member has received twenty percent (20%) of the amounts distributed under Tiers 2 and 3.

    TIER 4 - Residual Split:
    Thereafter, eighty percent (80%) to Investor and twenty percent (20%) to Managing Member.

4.2 Promote IRR Hurdles. Notwithstanding Section 4.1, the distribution percentages in Tier 4 shall be adjusted as follows:

    (a) If Investor achieves a fifteen percent (15%) IRR:
        Seventy percent (70%) to Investor, thirty percent (30%) to Managing Member

    (b) If Investor achieves a twenty percent (20%) IRR:
        Sixty-five percent (65%) to Investor, thirty-five percent (35%) to Managing Member

ARTICLE 5 - MANAGEMENT

5.1 Managing Member Authority. Managing Member shall have exclusive authority to manage the day-to-day operations of the Company and the Project, including:
    (a) Construction management
    (b) Property management
    (c) Leasing
    (d) Operating budgets under $500,000

5.2 Major Decisions. The following require unanimous Member approval:
    (a) Sale or refinancing of the Project
    (b) Capital calls exceeding the approved budget
    (c) Modifications to Project plans materially affecting scope or cost
    (d) Execution of construction contracts exceeding $5,000,000
    (e) Admission of additional Members
    (f) Bankruptcy filings
    (g) Related party transactions

ARTICLE 6 - FEES

6.1 Development Fee. Managing Member shall receive a development fee equal to four percent (4%) of total hard and soft costs, payable pro rata with construction draws.

6.2 Asset Management Fee. Managing Member shall receive an annual asset management fee equal to one-half percent (0.5%) of total Project cost, payable monthly.

6.3 Property Management Fee. Managing Member or its affiliate shall receive a property management fee equal to three percent (3%) of gross revenues.

6.4 Disposition Fee. Managing Member shall receive a disposition fee equal to one percent (1%) of gross sales price upon sale of the Project.

ARTICLE 7 - REMOVAL OF MANAGING MEMBER

7.1 Cause Events. Managing Member may be removed for Cause upon:
    (a) Fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence
    (b) Material breach of this Agreement not cured within 30 days
    (c) Bankruptcy or insolvency of Managing Member
    (d) Indictment of Managing Member principals for felony offenses

7.2 Effect of Removal. Upon removal for Cause:
    (a) Managing Member's promote shall be reduced to zero
    (b) Managing Member's Capital Account shall be redeemed at 50% of fair market value

ARTICLE 8 - TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

8.1 Prohibited Transfers. No Member may transfer its Interest without the prior written consent of the other Members.

8.2 Right of First Offer. Any Member desiring to sell must first offer its Interest to the other Members at fair market value.

8.3 Tag-Along Rights. If Managing Member receives a bona fide offer for its Interest, Investor may elect to include a pro rata portion of its Interest in such sale on the same terms.

8.4 Drag-Along Rights. If Investor desires to sell its Interest after Year 5, Investor may require Managing Member to sell its Interest on the same terms.

ARTICLE 9 - GOVERNING LAW

9.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware.
