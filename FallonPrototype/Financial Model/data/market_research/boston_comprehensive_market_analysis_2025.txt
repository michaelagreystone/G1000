BOSTON METROPOLITAN AREA COMPREHENSIVE REAL ESTATE MARKET ANALYSIS
================================================================================
Prepared: February 2025 | Data Sources: CBRE, Cushman & Wakefield, Marcus & Millichap,
Newmark, CoStar, HUD, Boston Pads, Matthews Real Estate, Yardi Matrix
================================================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Boston remains one of the most resilient and sought-after real estate markets in
the United States, characterized by supply constraints, strong institutional
employment anchors (healthcare, biotech, education, financial services), and
premium pricing. The city's multifamily sector shows signs of stabilization after
pandemic-era volatility, while the office market continues to navigate structural
changes from hybrid work adoption.

4.3 LABOR MARKET CONDITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Boston is a strong union market requiring union labor
- Labor costs 15-20% higher than national average
- Skilled trade shortage ongoing
- Union agreements provide predictable cost escalation
- Prevailing wage requirements on public projects

4.4 SOFT COST FACTORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Soft costs: 28-30% of hard costs (higher than national 20-25%)
- Permitting complexity adds time and cost
- Environmental review requirements
- Affordable housing linkage fees
- Historical preservation requirements in some areas

- Private equity increasing allocations to Boston

5.3 FINANCING CONDITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Construction LTC: 55-60% (lower than national due to high costs)
- Construction loan spread: SOFR + 250-300bps
- Permanent financing: Available for stabilized assets
- CMBS market recovering for quality assets
- Agency (Fannie/Freddie) active in multifamily

- Competition from lower-cost Sunbelt markets

SECTION 7: FORECAST AND RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

7.1 MULTIFAMILY OUTLOOK (2025-2027)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Rent growth: 2-3% annually (below historical 3.5% due to supply)
- Vacancy: Expected to decline from 6.2% to 5.5% as pipeline thins
- Cap rates: Stable at current levels; compression possible if rates cut
- Best opportunities: Value-add in Dorchester, Roxbury, East Boston

7.2 OFFICE OUTLOOK (2025-2027)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Vacancy: May peak at 23-24% before stabilizing
- Rents: Class A premium maintains; Class B/C pressure continues
- Conversions: Will remove obsolete stock from market
- Best opportunities: Class A assets in Seaport, Financial District

7.3 DEVELOPMENT RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Focus on multifamily in supply-constrained submarkets
- Consider mixed-use with ground-floor life sciences/lab
- Build to net-zero carbon standards (regulatory requirement coming)
- Plan for 18-24 month lease-up on new development
- Budget for Boston's higher soft costs and union labor
