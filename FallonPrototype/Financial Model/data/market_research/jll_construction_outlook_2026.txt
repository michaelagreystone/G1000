JLL 2026 U.S. Construction Perspective - Key Insights
Source: JLL Research, December 2025
===============================================================

CONSTRUCTION COST TRENDS
------------------------
The construction market faces deepening divisions with rising complexity.

Cost Indices (2026 Forecast):
- National construction cost index: +3.5-4.5% YoY
- Labor costs: +4.0-5.0% YoY (skilled trades shortage)
- Materials: +2.0-3.5% YoY (supply chain normalized)
- Concrete/steel: Stable to +2%
- Lumber: Volatile, +/-5%

Regional Construction Cost Factors (vs National Avg):
- Boston Metro: 1.35x (union labor, permitting)
- Charlotte Metro: 0.92x (right-to-work, efficient permitting)
- Nashville Metro: 0.95x (moderate labor costs)

DEVELOPMENT COSTS BY PRODUCT TYPE
---------------------------------

Multifamily (Wood-Frame, 4-5 Story):
- National Average: $285-325/SF
- Southeast Markets: $250-290/SF
- Northeast Markets: $350-425/SF
- Excludes land, soft costs

Multifamily (Concrete/Steel High-Rise):
- National Average: $425-525/SF
- Gateway Markets: $500-650/SF

Office (Class A):
- National Average: $350-450/SF
- Boston/NYC: $550-700/SF
- Southeast: $325-400/SF

Select-Service Hotel:
- National Average: $175,000-225,000/key
- Urban Markets: $225,000-300,000/key
- Limited-Service: $125,000-175,000/key

SUPPLY PIPELINE STATUS
----------------------
- U.S. office under construction: 20% below 2011 historic lows
- Groundbreakings declined from 2024's record lows
- Multifamily starts Q4 2025: Lowest since 2019
- Hotel construction subdued due to financing challenges

FINANCING ENVIRONMENT
---------------------
Construction Loan Rates (Q1 2026):
- SOFR: 3.75-4.00%
- Multifamily Spread: 250-325 bps (all-in 6.25-7.25%)
- Office Spread: 325-400 bps (all-in 7.00-8.00%)
- Hotel Spread: 375-450 bps (all-in 7.50-8.50%)

Lender Activity:
- Regional banks returning cautiously
- Life companies active for stabilized assets
- Debt funds filling gaps for construction
- Agency lending (Fannie/Freddie) at 20% market share

Underwriting Standards:
- Debt yield minimum: 8-9%
- DSCR minimum: 1.25x (multifamily), 1.30x (office)
- LTC: Down 5-10% from peak
- Recourse increasingly required for sponsors without track record

TIMELINE CONSIDERATIONS
-----------------------
Typical Development Timeline (Ground-Up):
- Entitlements: 6-18 months (market dependent)
- Design/Permits: 4-8 months
- Construction (Multifamily 200 units): 18-24 months
- Construction (Office 150K SF): 20-26 months
- Construction (Hotel 200 keys): 18-22 months
- Lease-Up (Multifamily): 12-18 months
- Lease-Up (Office): 18-30 months
- Hotel Ramp-Up: 12-18 months to stabilization

LABOR MARKET
------------
- Skilled trades shortage continues nationwide
- Electricians, plumbers in highest demand
- Immigration policy impacting workforce availability
- Wage pressure expected to persist through 2026
- Productivity gains from technology adoption modest

RECOMMENDATIONS FOR DEVELOPERS
------------------------------
1. Lock in pricing early with GMP contracts
2. Build contingency buffers of 8-10%
3. Consider modular/prefab for cost efficiency
4. Prioritize markets with favorable permitting
5. Maintain strong lender relationships
6. Prepare for extended timelines in gateway markets
