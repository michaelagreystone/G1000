EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The U.S. commercial real estate capital markets are navigating a transitional
period characterized by stabilizing interest rates, recovering transaction
volume, and bifurcated property performance. Construction costs have moderated
from 2022-2023 peaks but remain elevated. The multifamily sector continues to
attract the most capital despite near-term supply headwinds.

5.3 DEVELOPMENT SPREAD ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Development spread = Yield on Cost - Exit Cap Rate
Healthy spread: 100-200 bps minimum to justify development risk

Current Environment Assessment:
- Multifamily: Spreads compressed (75-125 bps typical)
- Office: Negative spreads in most markets (development paused)
- Hotel: Spreads vary widely by segment (100-200 bps luxury)
- Industrial: Healthy spreads maintained (125-175 bps)

SECTION 7: INVESTMENT RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

7.1 MULTIFAMILY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BUY: Value-add opportunities in 1990-2010 vintage
HOLD: Core assets in supply-constrained markets
SELL: Non-core assets in highest supply markets

Target Markets: Charlotte, Nashville, Boston, Raleigh, Denver
Avoid: Austin (oversupply), Phoenix (supply peak), most gateway CBDs

7.2 OFFICE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BUY: Trophy assets at distressed pricing (<$400/SF in gateway)
HOLD: Class A with long-term credit tenancy
SELL: Class B/C suburban without conversion potential

Strategy: Highly selective; flight to quality continuing

7.3 HOTEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BUY: Distressed select-service in recovering markets
HOLD: Well-positioned luxury/lifestyle assets
SELL: Economy assets in oversupplied markets

Target Markets: Coastal leisure, major convention cities
Avoid: Secondary markets with heavy supply (Nashville near-term)

7.4 DEVELOPMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Favorable: Industrial, multifamily (2026 start), life sciences
Pause: Office (except trophy), hotel (most markets)
Opportunity: Adaptive reuse/conversion plays
