CBRE U.S. Cap Rate Survey H2 2025 - Key Findings
Source: CBRE Research, February 2026
===============================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
The CBRE U.S. Cap Rate Survey for H2 2025 indicates that cap rates have 
stabilized across most property types, with industry professionals suggesting 
that the peak in yields has likely passed. Transaction activity is rebounding 
as buyer-seller alignment improves.

MULTIFAMILY CAP RATES (Class A Stabilized)
------------------------------------------
- National Average: 5.25%
- Charlotte, NC: 4.75-5.00% (compressed vs. 2024)
- Nashville, TN: 5.00-5.25% 
- Boston Seaport: 4.50-4.75% (gateway premium)
- Southeast secondary markets: 5.25-5.75%

Key Insight: Multifamily pricing is considered appropriate by most respondents.
Cap rates have remained largely unchanged, with many believing yields have peaked.

OFFICE CAP RATES (Class A CBD)
------------------------------
- National Average: 6.50%
- Boston Seaport: 5.75-6.25%
- Charlotte Uptown: 6.50-7.00%
- Nashville Downtown: 6.25-6.75%

Key Insight: Sentiment around office is improving, signaling potential turning 
point. Flight to quality continues with premium for new construction.

HOTEL CAP RATES (Select-Service)
--------------------------------
- National Average: 7.25%
- Nashville Market: 7.00-7.50%
- Boston Metro: 7.00-7.25%
- Charlotte Area: 7.25-7.75%

Key Insight: Select-service segment facing ADR pressure in 2025-2026.

CONSTRUCTION LOAN ENVIRONMENT (Q1 2026)
---------------------------------------
- 1-Month SOFR: 3.99% (down from 4.72% YoY)
- Typical Spread for Multifamily: 275-325 bps
- Typical Spread for Office: 325-400 bps
- Typical Spread for Hotel: 350-425 bps
- All-in Rate Range: 6.50-8.25%

LTC Ratios:
- Multifamily: 60-65%
- Office: 55-60%
- Hotel: 50-55%

MARKET OUTLOOK 2026
-------------------
- Cap rates expected to gradually compress 25-50 bps through 2026
- Transaction volume projected +35% vs 2025
- Construction pipeline at historic lows - favorable for existing assets
- Industrial and multifamily remain most favored sectors
- Distress levels remain elevated but showing signs of decline

REGIONAL HIGHLIGHTS
-------------------
Charlotte:
- Population growth continues driving apartment demand
- Northern corridor (Cornelius, Huntersville) facing supply pressure
- South End/Uptown seeing reduced new supply, stronger rent growth
- Construction starts at lowest since 2019

Nashville:
- 80,000+ new residents since 2022
- 20-34 age demographic up 3.4% vs 0.9% national
- Unemployment at 2.8% - among lowest major metros
- 27,000 new households formed
- Mixed-use development remains active downtown

Boston:
- Supply-constrained market supporting premium rents
- Seaport submarket commanding Class A premium
- Union labor costs ~35% above national average
- Permitting complexity extends timelines 6-12 months
