CHARLOTTE METROPOLITAN AREA COMPREHENSIVE REAL ESTATE MARKET ANALYSIS
================================================================================
Prepared: February 2025 | Data Sources: CBRE, Cushman & Wakefield, Marcus & Millichap,
Yardi Matrix, RealPage Analytics, Avison Young, Northmarq, Institutional Property Advisors
================================================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Charlotte has emerged as one of the top growth markets in the United States,
driven by robust population influx, corporate relocations, and a favorable
business environment. The multifamily market has absorbed record supply while
maintaining occupancy above 91%. The city is transitioning from a supply-heavy
cycle to a more balanced environment expected in 2026-2027.

SECTION 4: SUBMARKET DEEP DIVE - UPTOWN
================================================================================

4.1 OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Uptown (Charlotte's CBD) is the financial center of the Southeast, home to:
- Bank of America headquarters
- Wells Fargo East Coast hub
- Truist (BB&T/SunTrust merged HQ)
- Duke Energy headquarters

4.2 OFFICE MARKET CHARACTERISTICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Total inventory: ~25 million SF
- Class A inventory: 18 million SF
- Under construction: 0 SF (first time in decades)
- Flight to quality evident in leasing patterns

4.3 RESIDENTIAL DEVELOPMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Limited residential in core Uptown but surrounding areas seeing growth:
- First Ward: New high-rise development
- Fourth Ward: Historic renovation + new construction
- Gateway District: Mixed-use pipeline

SECTION 7: INVESTMENT OUTLOOK AND RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

7.1 MULTIFAMILY INVESTMENT THESIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHORT-TERM (2025): Challenging due to supply peak
- Rent growth: Flat to -1%
- Occupancy pressure: 8-9% vacancy market-wide
- Concession environment: 4-6 weeks free common

MEDIUM-TERM (2026-2027): Favorable recovery
- Supply declining sharply (76% fewer starts)
- Rent growth returning: 2-3% projected
- Occupancy improvement: Sub-7% vacancy expected
- Absorption outpacing supply for first time since 2021

LONG-TERM: Exceptional fundamentals
- Population growth sustaining 2.5%+ annually
- Job creation strong and diversified
- Affordability advantage vs. coastal markets
- Infrastructure investment (transit, airport)

7.2 RECOMMENDED STRATEGIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Value-Add Opportunities:
- 1990-2009 vintage properties in University, North Charlotte
- Interior renovation plays with $3,000-5,000/unit investment
- Amenity upgrades to compete with new supply
- Expected returns: 15-18% IRR with 2.0x+ equity multiple

Core-Plus Opportunities:
- 2015-2019 vintage in South End, South Charlotte
- Stable cash flow with modest upside
- Expected returns: 12-14% IRR with 1.8-2.0x equity multiple

Development:
- Wait for 2026 start for better supply/demand dynamics
- Focus on South End, University City, light rail corridors
- Target 14-16% levered IRR

7.3 RISK FACTORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Near-term oversupply (2025 peak deliveries)
- Bank of America office consolidation impact
- Interest rate sensitivity on transaction volume
- Competition with Atlanta, Raleigh for corporate relocations
- Housing affordability concerns limiting rent growth
