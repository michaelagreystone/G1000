U.S. Hotel Industry Outlook 2025-2026
Sources: STR, HVS, JLL Hospitality Research
===============================================================

INDUSTRY PERFORMANCE SUMMARY
----------------------------
2025 Results:
- RevPAR: $100.02 (-0.3% YoY) - first non-recessionary decline
- ADR: $160.54 (+0.9% YoY)
- Occupancy: 62.3% (-1.2% YoY)

2026 Forecast:
- RevPAR: +0.6% (modest recovery)
- ADR: +1.0% growth
- Demand: +0.4% rebound
- Supply: +1.2% growth

SEGMENT PERFORMANCE
-------------------
Luxury/Upper Upscale:
- Best positioned to maintain rates
- Strong group/business travel demand
- RevPAR premium widening vs. economy

Select-Service:
- ADR pressure continues
- RevPAR flat to slightly negative
- Cost efficiency critical for profitability
- Record RevPAR of $78 achieved in 2024

Extended-Stay:
- Outperforming traditional select-service
- Demand from remote workers, project-based stays
- Highest profit margins in segment

Economy/Midscale:
- Trade-down pressure from price-sensitive travelers
- Volatility in demand patterns
- Operating cost squeeze

DEVELOPMENT COSTS (Per Key)
---------------------------
Select-Service (New Construction):
- National Average: $175,000-225,000/key
- High-cost markets: $250,000-325,000/key
- Budget prototypes: $125,000-160,000/key

Full-Service:
- National Average: $350,000-450,000/key
- Gateway Markets: $500,000-700,000/key

Extended-Stay:
- National Average: $150,000-200,000/key
- Premium brands: $200,000-275,000/key

Key Cost Components:
- Land: 15-20% of total development cost
- Hard costs: 55-65%
- Soft costs: 15-20%
- FF&E: 8-12%
- Pre-opening: 2-4%

OPERATING METRICS
-----------------
Select-Service Typical Pro Forma:
- ADR: $140-180/night
- Occupancy: 68-72%
- RevPAR: $95-130/night
- GOP Margin: 38-45%
- NOI Margin: 28-35%
- Management Fee: 3% of revenue
- FF&E Reserve: 4% of revenue

Nashville Market Specifics:
- ADR: $195/night (upscale select-service)
- Stabilized Occupancy: 72%
- RevPAR: $140/night
- Exit Cap Rate: 7.00-7.50%

FINANCING
---------
Hotel Construction Loans (Q1 2026):
- LTC: 50-55% (lower than other CRE)
- Rate: SOFR + 375-450 bps
- All-in: 7.50-8.50%
- Term: 3 years + extensions
- Recourse common for new developers

Permanent Financing:
- Cap Rate Spread to Treasuries: 300-400 bps
- Typical Hold: 7-10 years for stabilization
- CMBS availability limited for hotels

INVESTMENT CONSIDERATIONS
-------------------------
Positive Factors:
- Record low construction starts
- Strong group/convention demand
- Business travel recovery
- FIFA World Cup boost (mid-2026)

Risk Factors:
- Economic uncertainty
- Consumer budget constraints
- Inflation impact on leisure travel
- Labor cost pressure
- ADR growth ceiling

Cap Rate Ranges (Stabilized):
- Select-Service: 7.00-8.00%
- Full-Service: 6.50-7.50%
- Extended-Stay: 6.75-7.50%
- Limited-Service: 8.00-9.00%
